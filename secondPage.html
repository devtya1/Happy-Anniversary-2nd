My Beloved Princess

My Beloved Princess Tinni,

I’m sitting here, thinking about all the moments we’ve shared—the laughter, the joy, and, yes, the arguments. And as I think about those arguments, my heart aches because I know I’ve caused you pain. I’ve hurt you in ways that I never should have, and I want you to know that I am deeply sorry for all the times I made you feel small, upset, or unloved.

You are the most important person in my life, and the last thing I ever want to do is hurt you. But sometimes, in the heat of the moment, I lose sight of that. I get so caught up in my own frustration or pride that I forget the love that binds us, the love that should always guide me. And when I hurt you, it’s like a part of me breaks, because you mean more to me than anything in this world.

I know I’ve made mistakes—more than I’d like to admit—and each one weighs on my heart. For every time I failed to be patient, for every moment I let anger take over, for every word I said that I should’ve never spoken, I am truly, deeply sorry.

You are my everything, my princess, my baccha, and I love you more than words could ever express.

Forever and always,
Your laddu
